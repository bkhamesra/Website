About Me

Hi, Welcome to my site!
I am Bhavesh, an astrophysics graduate student who dabbles with theory of general relativity to understand some of the (small) secrets of universe. I did my undergraduate and masters degree from Indian Institute of Science Education and Research where I had my first introduction to General Relativity. During my undergrad, I had the opportunity to work with Dr. Suneeta Vardarajan (IISER Pune), Prof. Jutta Kunz-Drolshegan and Dr. Saskia Grunau (University of Oldenburg) and Dr. Parameswaran Ajith (ICTS, Bengaluru) under whom I have explored different areas of GR including perturbation theory, higher dimensional theories of gravity and gravitational waves. To further pursue my interest in GR, I continued my studies at Center of Relativitic Astrophysics at Georgia Institute of Technology under the supervision of Prof. Pablo Laguna and Dr. Deirdre Shoemaker. Currently I am working in the area of numerical relativity. Using the numerical simulations I have focussed my research in understanding the astrophysics of compact binaries.
